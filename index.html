Sales Messaging Playbook

4-part framework for messaging that actually sells:

1. Observation (Research-Based Opening)

Show you've done homework. Lead with specific research about their company.

2. Connection (Turn Research Into Problem)

Connect observation → their likely challenge. Show you understand their problem.

3. Credibility (Proof You Can Help)

Similar customer example OR proof you've done this before.

4. Call to Conversation

Not "can we meet?" but "how are you handling that today?"

The Email Structure

[Observation - 1 line]
[Connection - 1 line]
[Credibility - 1 line]
[Call to conversation - 1 line]
[Closing - 1 line]
[Signature]

ICP Qualification Checklist

Score Your Prospect (5 minutes)

Total scoring: 0-100

  • Company Fundamentals (20 points) - Right industry, size, stage, not competitor
  • Problem Fit (20 points) - Has the problem, pain is real, tried to solve before, actively looking
  • Decision Making (15 points) - Talking to decision maker, budget authority, clear timeline
  • Budget (15 points) - Can afford your price, budget approved, spent on similar before
  • Opportunity (15 points) - Timeline matches, they're serious, win probability is high

Scoring Guide

  • 70-80: Pursue aggressively
  • 60-69: Good prospect, move forward
  • 50-59: Maybe, see if something changes
  • <50: Pass

Red Flags (Pass Immediately)

  • "Just want to pick your brain"
  • "Need to talk to 5 other people"
  • "We'll revisit next year"
  • "Price shopping"
  • "Want free work to test you"

Cold Email Framework

Based on Lavender AI data: 40% reply rates (vs 2% industry average)

The 4-Part Structure

  1. Observation: One sentence showing specific research about them
  2. Connection: One sentence connecting observation to their problem
  3. Credibility: One sentence proving you can help (similar customer example)
  4. Call to Conversation: One sentence asking an easy question (not "can we meet?")

Critical Rules

  • Keep it under 50 words (83% more replies)
  • Use 3rd-5th grade reading level (67% more replies)
  • Be personal with one specific detail
  • Show problem understanding, not your solution
  • Remove clichés ("Hope this finds you well" = auto-delete)
  • Mobile first (one sentence per line)

What Not to Do

  • Long emails
  • Jargon ("synergy," "leverage," "innovative")
  • Generic openings
  • Your story (they don't care yet)
  • Product pitch
  • Images or links
