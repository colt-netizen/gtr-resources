# Copy Skill - Cold Email Framework

**Based on Lavender AI data: 40% reply rates (vs 2% industry average)**

---

## The Framework (4 Parts)

### 1. OBSERVATION (Research-Based Opening)
**Why:** Shows you've done homework. Opens email without being spam.
**How:** One sentence max. Lead with specific research about their company.

```
"I saw you hired 3 new GTM people in Nashville" 
"You just released a Series A announcement"
"Your company switched to HubSpot recently"
```

**Not:** "I hope this email finds you well" or "I came across your profile"

---

### 2. CONNECTION (Turn Research Into Problem)
**Why:** Research alone means nothing. You need to show you understand their *problem*.
**How:** One sentence. Connect observation → their likely challenge.

```
Observation: "You hired 3 GTM people"
Connection: "I'm guessing onboarding them efficiently is a priority"

Observation: "Series A announcement" 
Connection: "That means repeatable sales process is suddenly critical"
```

---

### 3. CREDIBILITY (Proof You Can Help)
**Why:** Establishes why you should be heard.
**How:** Similar customer example OR one-sentence proof.

```
"We helped [company like theirs] reduce onboarding time by 40%"
"Most teams we work with see X outcome"
"[Well-known customer] uses this approach"
```

**Not:** Buzzwords, jargon, or random features

---

### 4. CALL TO CONVERSATION (Not CTA)
**Why:** You don't ask for 30 minutes. You ask to start a conversation.
**How:** Open-ended question that's easy to answer.

```
❌ "Are you open to a 15-minute call?"
❌ "Can we schedule a meeting?"
✅ "How are you handling that today?"
✅ "Is that a priority for you right now?"
✅ "Worth a quick conversation?"
```

## The Email Structure (In Action)

```
Subject: [2-3 words, boring, sounds internal]

Body:

[Observation - 1 line]

[Connection - 1 line]

[Credibility - 1 line]

[Call to conversation - 1 line]

[Closing - 1 line]

[Signature]
```

### Example Email

```
Subject: Onboarding GTM hires

Hi [Name],

I saw you're scaling your Nashville GTM team. 

Ramping new reps efficiently is usually a blocker for growth teams.

We helped DuckDuckGo cut their onboarding from 8 weeks to 5.

How are you handling it?

[Your name]
```

**Word count:** 41 (perfect)
**Reading level:** 4th grade (perfect)
**Mobile optimized:** One line per idea (perfect)
**Lavender score:** ~92/100

## Critical Rules

### ✅ DO THIS

- **Keep it short:** 25-50 words ideal. Max 75.
- **Use white space:** One sentence per line.
- **Be simple:** 3rd-5th grade reading level (67% more replies)
- **Be personal:** Their name + specific detail about them/company
- **Show problem understanding:** Not your solution, their pain
- **Ask easy questions:** Yes/no or specific, not open-ended
- **Remove clichés:** "Hope this finds you well" = auto-delete
- **Mobile first:** 90% read on phone, breaks at every line
- **Use strong subject:** Boring > Clever (Neutral tone, 2-3 words, no punctuation)

### ❌ DON'T DO THIS

- **Long emails:** Every extra sentence = fewer replies
- **Jargon:** "Synergy," "leverage," "innovative," "game-changing" = spam folder
- **No personalization:** Generic = deleted
- **Your story:** They don't care about you yet
- **Product pitch:** No benefits, features, pricing
- **Clichés:** Every one drops reply rates 5-10%
- **Questions:** Avoid yes/no if they have options
- **Buzzwords:** AI, cutting-edge, revolutionary = spam trigger
- **Images/links:** Deliverability killer (unless proven needed)

## Scoring System (Lavender-Aligned)

**90+ = Ship it (high reply probability)**
- ✅ Short (<50 words)
- ✅ Personalized 
- ✅ Shows problem understanding
- ✅ Easy to reply to
- ✅ No clichés
- ✅ Simple language

**70-89 = Good, but refinable**
- ⚠️ Too long (reduce 20%)
- ⚠️ Generic phrase somewhere
- ⚠️ Jargon detected
- ⚠️ Not specific enough

**<70 = Rewrite**
- ❌ >100 words (cut 50%)
- ❌ No personalization
- ❌ Unclear why you're emailing
- ❌ Asking for meeting not conversation

- Company funding round
- Hiring announcement

- LinkedIn activity

"If they did X, they probably have problem Y"

### Step 3: Add Credibility
Similar customer story OR quick proof

### Step 4: Ask Conversation Starter
Not "can you meet?" but "how are you handling that?"

### Step 5: Score Yourself

- 3rd-5th grade reading level? ✅
- Personalized? ✅
- No jargon? ✅

## What Gets Filtered Out (Avoid These)

**Spam triggers:**
- "Free trial"
- "Limited offer"
- "Act now"
- "Investment opportunity"
- "Excited to connect" (cliché)
- "I hope this finds you well" (cliché)
- Multiple images/links
- ALL CAPS
- Too many exclamation marks!!!

**Jargon red flags:**
- "Synergy," "leverage," "innovative," "disruptive"
- "Help you grow exponentially"
- "Cutting-edge solution"
- "Game-changing"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Thought leader"

**Formatting killers:**
- Paragraphs (keep to 1-2 sentences max)
- Long sentences (if it takes a breath to read, split it)
- No white space on phone
- Tiny text or non-mobile font

## Follow-Up Email (If No Reply)

Same rules apply. But add:
- Reference to first email
- More specific insight
- Easier to reply

```
Subject: [Previous subject - Re: line]

Hi [First name],

Just following up on my note about [specific thing].

[New insight or different angle]

Worth chatting?

[Name]
```

**Rule:** Max 2-3 follow-ups. After that, move on.

## The Mindset

**Write for a 30-second scan.** Your prospect has 11 seconds to read. Make every word count.

**Be useful, not impressive.** Jargon makes you sound smart to people who don't care. Simple makes you sound trustworthy.

**They don't care about you yet.** Focus on their problem, not your solution.

**Conversation > Pitch.** The sale comes later. First goal: reply.

**Test and iterate.** A/B test subject lines. Track replies. Do more of what works.
